## What are Double-Pane Windows?
Double-pane windows, also known as insulated glass units (IGUs), consist of two panes of glass separated by a gap. This gap is usually filled with an inert gas like argon or krypton, which helps to reduce heat transfer and minimize noise pollution.
## Benefits of Double-Pane Windows
The advantages of double-pane windows are numerous. Some of the key benefits include:
* **Energy Efficiency**: Double-pane windows can significantly reduce heat loss during winter and heat gain during summer, resulting in lower energy bills and a reduced carbon footprint.
* **Noise Reduction**: The gap between the two panes of glass helps to absorb sound, making double-pane windows an excellent choice for homes located near busy roads or airports.
* **Condensation Reduction**: The inert gas filling the gap between the panes helps to reduce condensation, which can lead to mold and mildew growth.
* **Increased Security**: Double-pane windows are more difficult to break than single-pane windows, making them a safer choice for homes with young children or pets.
