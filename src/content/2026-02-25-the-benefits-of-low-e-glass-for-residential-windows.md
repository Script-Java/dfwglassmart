# Introduction to Low-E Glass
Low-E glass, or low emissivity glass, is a type of energy-efficient glass that is designed to reduce heat transfer between the inside and outside of a building. This is achieved through a special coating that is applied to the glass, which reflects heat rather than absorbing it.
## Benefits of Low-E Glass
* Reduced energy bills: By reducing heat transfer, Low-E glass can help to keep your home warmer in the winter and cooler in the summer, which can lead to significant savings on your energy bills.
* Increased comfort: Low-E glass can also help to reduce drafts and heat loss, making your home feel more comfortable and cozy.
* UV protection: The coating on Low-E glass can also block out UV rays, which can help to prevent fading and damage to your furniture and carpets.
## How Low-E Glass Works
Low-E glass works by using a special coating that is applied to the glass. This coating is typically made up of a thin layer of metal or metal oxide, which is applied to the glass using a process called sputtering. The coating is designed to reflect heat rather than absorbing it, which helps to reduce heat transfer between the inside and outside of the building.
## Conclusion
Low-E glass is a highly effective way to improve the energy efficiency of your home. By reducing heat transfer and blocking out UV rays, Low-E glass can help to save you money on your energy bills and make your home feel more comfortable and cozy.
